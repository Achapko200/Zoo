Monkeys

Monkeys are intelligent and social animals known for their agility and playful behavior. They are found in various habitats worldwide, from tropical rainforests to savannas. Monkeys are known for their expressive faces, dexterous hands, and various vocalizations. They live in groups called troops and have complex social structures. Their diet mainly consists of fruits, leaves, and insects, varying depending on the species.

Lemur

Lemurs are primates native to the island of Madagascar. They are known for their large, reflective eyes, bushy tails, and highly social lifestyle. Lemurs are unique to Madagascar and contribute significantly to its biodiversity.

Lama

Lamas are domesticated South American camelids widely known for their gentle demeanor and versatility. Native to the Andean region, they have been used for centuries as pack animals and for their soft wool, made into textiles. Llamas are social creatures that live in herds and communicate with various vocalizations, from gentle hums to alarm calls. giraffe

Giraffes are the tallest land animals, known for their long necks, legs, and distinctive spotted patterns. Native to Africa, they inhabit savannas and open woodlands, where they browse leaves, flowers, and fruits from high tree branches, especially acacias. A giraffe's height gives it a unique vantage point to spot predators and find food. Despite their size, giraffes are graceful and can run up to 35 miles per hour over short distances. They have a gentle disposition and are social animals, often found in loosely structured herds. Giraffes communicate with each other using a variety of sounds, including hums, grunts and snorts.
